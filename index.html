What is ReEntry Job Translator?

It is a simple tool that helps you describe your work experience better.

You type in what you actually did, such as kitchen work, laundry, cleaning, maintenance, inventory, clerical work, tutoring, or peer support. The tool rewrites that experience into professional resume language an employer can understand.

Why this matters

A lot of people have real work experience, but the words used inside institutions do not always sound like job experience on the outside.

It removes confusing labels

Instead of using words that might not help you, it turns your role into language that fits a normal resume.

It shows the skills behind the work

Food service, cleaning, maintenance, inventory, and peer support all involve real skills employers care about.

It helps you apply faster

You get resume-ready text you can copy, print, save as a PDF, or use when filling out job applications.

FAQs:

Is this really free?

Yes. Completely free for individuals. No hidden costs, no premium version, no upsells. We believe everyone deserves a fair shot at employment.

Do I need to create an account?

No. You can use the tool anonymously without any login. We only ask for your name and contact info if you want them on your actual resume.

What if I don't have much computer experience?

We designed this for people who might not be tech-savvy. If you can fill out a form, you can use this tool. It's mobile-friendly too, so you can do it from your phone.

Will employers know about my background?

Your resume won't mention incarceration directly. We translate institutional experience into standard job descriptions. Whether and when to disclose your background is your choice.

Can case managers use this with their clients?

Absolutely. Case managers love this tool because it saves time and produces better results than manual resume writing. Agencies can also license organizational features.
